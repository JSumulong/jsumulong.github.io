Get out of the country they said. It'll be good for you they said.

They were right!

In 2013 I jumped on a plane and left the country for the first time. I met a bunch of cool people and visited all of the awesome archaeological sites. We went all over the country and a couple of times we even went over to Jordan. Here are some details about how my 6 months abroad took shape:

Location Amount of Time Spent in Location Studies
Jerusalem 4 months Historical Geography, Archaeology, Culture and Literature of Israel and Jordan w/ field studies throughout Israel and Jordan
Ashkelon 2 months Archaeological Dig

Proof of the pudding: Check out this photo!

Israel and Jordan may not show up on the list of top countries to visit, but they are pretty awesome and they have a lot to offer. Here are a couple of reasons:

  1. History - There are thousands of years worth of ruins and archaeological sites to visit. And some of the greatest artifacts to belonging to many of the worlds oldest civilizations are housed in museums in Israel and Jordan!
  2. Cultural Exposure - Find out what it's like to haggle with the shop keepers in the Old City of Jerusalem. Enjoy some rare Druze cuisine in northern Galilee. Wander through the deserts of Jordan with the Bedouin and sleep under the stars. Learn about the Israeli/Palestinian conflict first hand! Don't let the news outlets determine your bias; go find out for yourself!
  3. Fun - Yes, it's also fun! Go snorkeling in Eilat or hiking on Mount Tabor or enjoy the hot springs of Sataf! You can even float in the Dead Sea!
